The **Early Eocene Climatic Optimum** (**EECO**), also referred to as the **Early Eocene Thermal Maximum** (**EETM**),[^christopherrobertscotese-1] was a period of extremely warm [greenhouse](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_and_icehouse_Earth "Greenhouse and icehouse Earth") climatic conditions during the [Eocene](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eocene "Eocene") [epoch](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epoch "Epoch"). The EECO represented the hottest sustained interval of the [Cenozoic](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cenozoic "Cenozoic") era and one of the hottest periods in all of Earth's history.[^woodburnegunnellstucky2009-2]

The EECO lasted from about 54 to 49 Ma.[^christopherrobertscotese-1] The EECO's onset is signified by a major geochemical enrichment in isotopically light carbon, commonly known as a negative [*δ*<sup>13</sup>C](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%9413C "Δ13C") excursion, that demarcates the [hyperthermal](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperthermal_event "Hyperthermal event") Eocene Thermal Maximum 3 (ETM3).[^3]

Following some [climate models](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_model "Climate model"), the EECO was marked by an extremely high [global mean surface temperature](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_surface_temperature "Global surface temperature"),[^christopherrobertscotese-1] which has been estimated to be anywhere between 23.2 and 29.7 °C, with the mean estimate being around 27.0 °C.[^4] In [North America](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America "North America"), the mean annual temperature was 23.0 °C, while the continent's overall mean annual precipitation (MAP) was about 1500 mm.[^woodburnegunnellstucky2009-2] The mean annual temperature range (MATR) of North America may have been as low as 47 °C or as high as 61 °C, while the MATR of [Asia](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia") was anywhere from 51 to 60 °C.[^5] The [Okanagan](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okanagan_Valley "Okanagan Valley") Highlands had a moist [mesothermal](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesothermal "Mesothermal") climate, with bioclimatic analysis of the region yielding estimates of a mean annual temperature (MAT) of 12.7-16.6 °C, a cold month mean temperature (CMMT) of 3.5-7.9 °C, and a MAP of 103-157 cm.[^6] [Clumped isotope](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clumped_isotopes "Clumped isotopes") measurements from the [Green River Basin](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_River_Basin "Green River Basin") and the [Bighorn Basin](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bighorn_Basin "Bighorn Basin") confirm a high seasonality of temperature, contradicting climatological predictions of an equable climate under greenhouse conditions.[^7][^8] Lake temperatures in the Green River Formation ranged from 28 °C to 35 °C,[^9] with lacustrine photic zone euxinia being prevalent.[^10] Sediments from [San Diego County, California](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Diego_County,_California "San Diego County, California") record a MAP of 1100 ± 299 mm, notably drier than the region was during the [Palaeocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleocene%E2%80%93Eocene_Thermal_Maximum "Paleocene–Eocene Thermal Maximum").[^11] [Sea surface temperatures](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature "Sea surface temperature") (SSTs) off of Seymour Island were ~15 °C.[^12] The high elevation areas of Asia, [Africa](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa"), and [Antarctica](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica") saw elevation dependent warming (EDW), while those in North America and [India](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_subcontinent "Indian subcontinent") saw [elevation dependent cooling](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Elevation_dependent_cooling&action=edit&redlink=1 "Elevation dependent cooling (page does not exist)") (EDC).[^13]

The latitudinal climate gradient is generally believed to have been smaller, which was mainly the result of a decrease in albedo differences across Earth's surface.[^14] Although SSTs are often believed to have had a shallow latitudinal temperature gradient, this is likely to be an artefact of burial-induced oxygen isotope reequilibration in [fossilised](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil "Fossil") benthic foraminifera.[^15]

Climate modelling [simulations](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simulation "Simulation") point to a [carbon dioxide concentration in the atmosphere](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_in_Earth%27s_atmosphere "Carbon dioxide in Earth's atmosphere") of about 1,680 ppm to reproduce the observed hothouse conditions of the EECO,[^deepsea1680ppm-16] although [geochemical proxies](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proxy_\(climate\) "Proxy (climate)") suggest only 700-900 ppm.[^17] Stomatal density in *[Gingko](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ginkgo "Ginkgo")* leaves suggests *p*CO<sub>2</sub> was over twice that of preindustrial levels.[^18] Additionally, [methane](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methane "Methane") concentrations in the [Early Eocene](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ypresian "Ypresian") may have been significantly higher than in the present day.[^19]

The nature of the hydrological cycle during the EECO is controversial. Evidence from German peat bogs suggests that it was highly variable, with alternations between aridity and humidity.[^20] Hydroclimatic variability in the [Gonjo basin](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gonjo_basin&action=edit&redlink=1 "Gonjo basin (page does not exist)"), Tibet, was predominantly controlled by orbital eccentricity cycles.[^21] Evidence from North America, in contrast, suggests that the hydrological cycle was enhanced during the EECO, although it remained relatively stable, unlike during the earlier hyperthermals, and that the stable hydroclimate may ultimately have ended the EECO by enabling high rates of organic carbon burial in lacustrine settings.[^22]

The EECO was preceded by a major long-term warming trend in the [Late Palaeocene](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Paleocene "Late Paleocene") and [Early Eocene](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ypresian "Ypresian").[^23] It was initiated by a series of intense hyperthermal events in the Early Eocene, including [Eocene Thermal Maximum 2](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eocene_Thermal_Maximum_2 "Eocene Thermal Maximum 2") (ETM2) and ETM3.[^24]

The emplacement of the Pana Formation, a [volcanic rock](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_rock "Volcanic rock") [formation](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geological_formation "Geological formation") in southern [Tibet](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet "Tibet") that may represent the product of a [supereruption](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supervolcano "Supervolcano"), has also been proposed as a source of excess carbon flux into the atmosphere that drove the EECO.[^25] Other research attributes the elevated greenhouse gas levels to increased generation of petroleum in sedimentary basins and enhanced ventilation of marine carbon.[^26]

The final phase of the [Angiosperm Terrestrial Revolution](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretaceous_Terrestrial_Revolution "Cretaceous Terrestrial Revolution") occurred during the EECO.[^benton2021-27] The supergreenhouse climate of the EECO fostered extensive floral diversification and increased habitat complexity in North American terrestrial biomes.[^woodburnegunnellstucky2009-2] The hot, humid conditions of the EECO may have facilitated the evolution of epiphytic [bryophytes](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryophyte "Bryophyte"), with the oldest member of [Lejeuneaceae](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lejeuneaceae "Lejeuneaceae") being described from fossils from the [Cambay amber](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cambay_amber&action=edit&redlink=1 "Cambay amber (page does not exist)") dating back to the EECO.[^28] The Okanagan Highlands in British Columbia and Washington became a biodiversity hotspot from which newly evolved lineages of temperate-adapted plants radiated from following the end of the EECO.[^29]

The climate was warm enough to allow palms and [palm beetles](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palm_beetle "Palm beetle") to inhabit upland regions of British Columbia and Washington.[^30] Ellesmere Island became inhabited by basal [primatomorphs](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primatomorph "Primatomorph"),[^31] while Canada's Western Arctic was home to crocodyliforms.[^32] The leadup to the EECO was marked by an increase in mammal diversity in Wyoming's [Bighorn Basin](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bighorn_Basin "Bighorn Basin").[^33] Across North America, the EECO's warmth and humidity promoted increased floral diversity and habitat complexity, which in turn increased mammalian diversity. The end of the EECO caused a drop in mammalian diversity known as the Bridgerian Crash.[^34]

Northern Yakutia was covered in mangroves.[^35] [Mongolia](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia "Mongolia") witnessed a humidification event that transformed it from a shrubland into a forest and significantly reducing local wildfire incidence.[^36]

In [South America](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America "South America"), the EECO coincided with the [Itaboraian](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Itaboraian "Itaboraian") [South American Land Mammal Age](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_American_land_mammal_age "South American land mammal age").[^37] [Cingulates](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cingulata "Cingulata") diversified over the course of the EECO.[^38]

In [Africa](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa"), the end of the EECO brought about a [hyaenodont](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyaenodonta "Hyaenodonta") turnover, with subfamilial richness among hyaenodonts increasing.[^39]

The northern margins of the [Australo-Antarctic Gulf](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Australo-Antarctic_Gulf&action=edit&redlink=1 "Australo-Antarctic Gulf (page does not exist)"), then located at 60-65 °S, were covered in wet-tropical lowland vegetation.[^40] *[Nypa](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nypa_\(genus\) "Nypa (genus)")* pollen is recorded in southeastern Australian sediments.[^41]

*Apectodinium* became abundant in the seas, and is often used as a biostratigraphic marker of the EECO.[^42] The central Tethys in what is now northeastern Italy was a hotspot of coral diversity, with its mesophotic deltaic environment acting as a refugium.[^43] At [Shatsky rise](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Shatsky_rise&action=edit&redlink=1 "Shatsky rise (page does not exist)"), the planktonic [foraminifera](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foraminifera "Foraminifera") *[Morozovella](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Morozovella&action=edit&redlink=1 "Morozovella (page does not exist)")* and *[Chiloguembelina](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Chiloguembelina&action=edit&redlink=1 "Chiloguembelina (page does not exist)")* declined in abundance. *[Acarinina](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acarinina "Acarinina")* became the dominant planktonic foraminifer in this locality.[^44] *Morozovella* underwent a switch from dextral to sinistral coiling across the EECO.[^45] The [euryhaline](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euryhaline "Euryhaline") [dinoflagellate](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinoflagellate "Dinoflagellate") *[Homotryblium](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Homotryblium&action=edit&redlink=1 "Homotryblium (page does not exist)")* became superabundant at the site of Waipara in [New Zealand](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand "New Zealand") during the early and middle EECO, reflecting the occurrence of significant stratification of surficial waters as well as increased salinity.[^46]

The EECO caused an increase in [chert](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chert "Chert") deposition by way of basin–basin fractionation by deep-sea circulation, causing increased silica concentrations in the North Atlantic which in turn resulted in direct precipitation of silica as well as its absorption by clay minerals.[^47] The Equatorial Pacific displays extensive chert deposits laid down during the EECO.[^48] The EECO was also marked by enhanced [glauconite](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glauconite "Glauconite") deposition.[^49]

## Comparison to present global warming

Because the [*p*CO<sub>2</sub>](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/PCO2 "PCO2") values of the EECO could potentially be reached if anthropogenic greenhouse gas emissions continue unabated for three centuries, the EECO has been used as an analogue for high-end projections of the Earth's future climate that would result from humanity's burning of fossil fuels.[^50] Based on the [Representative Concentration Pathway](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Representative_Concentration_Pathway "Representative Concentration Pathway") 8.5 (RCP8.5) emission scenario, by 2150 CE, the climates across much of the world would resemble conditions during the EECO.[^51] One scenario of Lee et. al. (2021) suggests that conditions comparable to EECO could occur by 2300 CE.[^lee2021-52]
